Demystifying Indian Stock Exchanges

About National Stock Exchange (NSE)

NSE stablished in 1992, the National Stock Exchange (NSE) has emerged as a cornerstone of India's financial landscape. It was founded with a specific objective: to bring transparency to the country’s capital market. NSE operates on a fully automated screen-based electronic trading system, setting new standards in efficiency and accessibility.

NSE played a pivotal role at the behest of the Indian government and the Pherwani Committee's recommendations. It represents a collaborative effort of leading financial institutions, fostering a dynamic and inclusive stock exchange environment.

NSE introduced electronic trading facilities from its inception, revolutionizing stock market operations. This technology-driven approach has provided investors with a fair, efficient, and transparent securities market.

As of April 11, 2023, NSE stands as a global leader with a substantial market capitalization, ranking among the largest stock exchanges in the world. The exchange's commitment to technological innovation and regulatory compliance has contributed to its reputation as a preferred platform for companies seeking listing.

Functions of NSE

The following are the primary functions of the National Stock Exchange –

Nationwide Trading Platform:

NSE was established to create a comprehensive trading platform that facilitates the buying and selling of equities, debt instruments, and hybrid securities on a national scale. This ensures that investors from all corners of the country can actively participate in capital market activities.

Equitable Access Through Communication Network:

The exchange is committed to providing equitable access to investors across the country. This is achieved through the establishment of a robust communication network, ensuring that investors, regardless of their geographical location, can efficiently access and engage in trading activities.

Facilitating Faster Settlement Cycles:

NSE aims to enable faster settlement cycles in the trading process. Through the implementation of book entry settlement systems, the exchange streamlines the settlement process, reducing the time required to complete transactions. This not only enhances operational efficiency but also aligns with the latest international norms and standards in securities markets.

About Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)

BSE established in 1875, the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) has played a pivotal role in shaping India's financial landscape. Initially, BSE operated as a Native Share and Stock Brokers' Association before transforming into a full-fledged stock exchange. Its establishment pre-dates NSE by almost a century.

Similar to NSE, BSE was a key player in the Pherwani Committee's recommendations, embodying the collaborative efforts of leading financial institutions. BSE stands as a testament to India's rich financial history, embracing a diverse range of shareholding assets from both global and domestic investors.

BSE holds the distinction of introducing the Sensex in 1986, India's first equity index. This index comprises 30 actively traded stocks, reflecting the overall market performance. The exchange has been a pioneer in adopting technological advancements, ensuring it remains at the forefront of providing efficient and transparent trading facilities.

As of April 11, 2023, the Bombay Stock Exchange boasts a significant market capitalization, contributing to its status as one of the largest stock exchanges globally. Its historical significance, coupled with a robust regulatory framework, has attracted a substantial number of listed companies.

Functions of BSE

The following are the primary functions of the Bombay Stock Exchange –

Price Determination through Valuation:

BSE constantly evaluates and values all the listed securities, taking into account various factors such as company performance, market trends, and investor sentiment. The index, popularly known as SENSEX, serves as a comprehensive indicator reflecting the overall market conditions, allowing investors to track the prices of various securities.

Contribution to the Economy:

By providing a robust trading platform for securities of numerous companies, BSE contributes significantly to the economy. The trading process involves continuous reinvestment and disinvestment, facilitating capital formation.

Facilitation of Liquidity:

One of the primary functions of BSE is to ensure a ready platform for the sale and purchase of securities, offering investors high liquidity. The continuous trading activities on the exchange enable investors to convert their existing securities into cash quickly. This liquidity feature is essential for investors, providing them with the confidence that they can execute transactions efficiently and at any time. It also contributes to the overall efficiency of the financial markets.

FAQ

How does NSE work?

The NSE starts its trading day with a pre-open session to discover the opening price of securities. The main trading session follows this.Investors place buy or sell orders through their brokerage accounts. These orders are then sent to the NSE’s electronic trading system.The NSE’s automated system matches buy and sell orders based on price and time priority. Once a match is found, the trade is executed. All trading activities and price changes are updated in real-time, providing constant information to investors. After the trading day ends, the NSE’s clearing corporation steps in to handle the clearing and settlement of all trades. This includes transferring the sold securities and the corresponding funds between the buying and selling parties.National Stock Exchange ensures compliance with all the regulatory requirements throughout this process. It employs robust risk management systems and surveillance mechanisms to monitor trading activities. Post-trading, NSE disseminates market data, including the closing prices of securities, trading volumes, and other relevant information, which is crucial for investors and market analysts. The market closes with a session that again determines the closing price of the securities based on the day’s trading activities.

How does BSE work?

The Bombay Stock Exchange uses the electronic trading platform to conduct its financial transactions. Through direct market access, investors can directly place market orders in BSE online without the need for external specialists. Later, the focus shifted from buyers/sellers to the total value of transactions in a day due to the lack of limit orders. Investors can trade on the BSE stock exchange through any brokerage firm against a fee. However, certain professional investors can apply for direct investment access who make large transactions on the BSE stock market. Also, the stock exchange uses BOLT – Bombay Online Trading Platform for efficient trading. Transactions on the BSE platform require T+2 rolling days for settlement, which means that all transactions take two days to process. Moreover, the SEBI is responsible for regulating the stock exchanges in India and constantly updating rules to ensure smooth operation.

How are stock prices determined?

Stock prices are determined by supply and demand in the market. When more people want to buy a stock (demand) than sell it (supply), the price tends to rise. Conversely, when more people want to sell a stock than buy it, the price tends to fall.If the supply is greater than the demand, the company’s share price will likely drop.

What is an index?

An index is a statistical measure of the performance of a group of stocks. It is used to represent the overall performance of the stock market or a specific sector of the market. Examples include the Nifty 50 and the Sensex.
